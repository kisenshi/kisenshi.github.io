Train

Mon Mar 30, 2015 in Concerts , Experiences using tags Concert , Train , experiences , o2Arena


If I tell you that Train was in London you might ask me who they are. You may not know them by name but I´m sure you have heard some of their most famous songs at least once: Hey, Soul Sister; Drive by; Drops of Jupiter… Still no clue? Please make yourself a favour and go to Spotify, Youtube, Google music, Itunes (or whatever you use to listen music), hit play (fall in love) and listen while reading!

The Californian band was formed in 1998 and they have released 7 albums since then; the last one, Bulletproof Picasso, is the reason why they are on tour now and I had the chance to see them live in the O2 Arena!

During almost two hours they went through all their albums performing a total of 20 songs! It was really cool listening all my favourite songs live in such an amazing place as the O2. (Yes, I was lucky and they played ALL my fav songs, which, in case you are curious, include Angel in blue jeans, Bruises, Save me San Francisco, Drive by and Drops of Jupiter, among others). It was my third concert in the O2 Arena and I can assure that it was, by far, the best one of them!

Lately, it´s trendy in the concerts that the bands try to interact with the attendees, taking advantage of mobiles and the well-known selfie fever and Train, being aware of this, got close with the fans reaching a level I hadn´t seen before. Pat (the singer) started giving away some free t-shirts by throwing them to the crowd (sadly, although I was really close, I was not lucky enough to take one), including one signed by the whole band! This last one was really valuable and it triggered some problems in the Arena and I heard that even security had to intervene (not sure what exactly happened or who took the t-shirt home as, honestly, I was more interested in the concert than gossiping!). After, he asked for phones in order to take selfies and provide some fans a good souvenir of the concert (if you are wondering, I don’t have a selfie either) and, after this, he jumped out the stage during a whole song to be closer to the fans and allow them to take pictures and sing with him!

The O2 was crowded (it is important to mention that it was a weekday Tuesday evening), the band gave their best and I’m sure they didn’t disappoint their fans (or at least not me!); I had a great time. Before saying goodbye, they even invited the two supporting bands, Natasha North and The magic numbers (a great discovering, and they deserve to be checked out as well), to join them in stage and perform a song with them. It was a great gesture and very well welcomed by everyone!

It was an amazing concert and they gave it closure performing Drops of Jupiter (obviously!); I really look forward to future concerts, maybe in San Francisco?! Dreaming is free!
